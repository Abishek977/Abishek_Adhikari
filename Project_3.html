Site Suitability Evaluation for New Health Facilities: Bharatpur, Nepal

Using geospatial technologies and MCDA to identify optimal sites for new health facilities in Bharatpur.

Project Overview

This project aims to identify optimal locations for new healthcare facilities in Bharatpur, Nepal, by employing Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA) combined with geospatial technologies. The study integrates factors such as population density, road accessibility, slope, proximity to existing hospitals, and land use/land cover (LULC) to create a composite suitability map that highlights the best potential sites for healthcare facilities.

Key Objectives

  • To assess spatial gaps in current healthcare service distribution.
  • To identify the most suitable locations for new health facilities using geospatial analysis.
  • To address accessibility issues in underserved areas of Bharatpur.

Data & Tools Used

Technologies and Methods

  • Geographic Information Systems (GIS): Data processing and spatial analysis.
  • Remote Sensing: Using Sentinel-2A imagery for LULC and slope analysis.
  • Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP): Pair-wise comparison and weighting of criteria.
  • ArcMap: Data cleaning, overlay analysis, and suitability mapping.

Data Sources

  • Sentinel-2A Imagery (10-meter resolution)
  • Digital Elevation Model (DEM)
  • Road and hospital data from the Humanitarian Data Exchange
  • Population density data from the Bharatpur municipality

Study Area

The study focuses on Bharatpur, located in Nepal's Chitwan District, with a population of 369,377 (2021 census). The area covers 433 kmÂ² and features a rapidly growing urban population that demands more healthcare services.

Analysis & Findings

Site Suitability Analysis

The analysis incorporated five criteria: population density, proximity to existing hospitals, slope, road accessibility, and land use/land cover (LULC). Using AHP and GIS tools, the criteria were weighted based on their importance, and a composite suitability map was generated.

Key Results

  • 13.25% of the study area was found to be unsuitable for new healthcare facilities.
  • 12.38% of the area was highly suitable, located in underserved regions lacking healthcare access.
  • The central region of Bharatpur, which is highly urbanized, shows the greatest need for new facilities.

Final Suitability Map

Conclusions & Recommendations

The study concludes that the current distribution of healthcare facilities in Bharatpur is imbalanced, with the central regions being well-served and peripheral areas underserved. The use of geospatial technologies and MCDA helped identify highly suitable locations for new facilities. It is recommended that new facilities be established in areas that have high population density but lack access to existing healthcare services. Strategic planning, based on geospatial data, is essential to improve healthcare access and reduce spatial inequality.
